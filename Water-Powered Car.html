Water-Powered Car

Daniel Dingel claimed to have invented a car that runs on water in the 1960s. Energy Efficiency: Water-powered cars, as they are often portrayed in popular culture, have not demonstrated practical energy efficiency. They would violate the first and second laws of thermodynamics, which govern energy conversion processes. Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicles: While water can be a source of hydrogen, the application is typically in the form of hydrogen fuel cells. Hydrogen fuel cell vehicles use hydrogen gas stored in high-pressure tanks, which reacts with oxygen from the air to generate electricity to power an electric motor. Water is the only byproduct of this process. However, this is not the same as using water as a direct fuel source.
