Moon Buggy

Invented by Eduardo San Juan in 1973 for NASA's Apollo 11 mission. Engineering Innovation: The Moon Buggy showcased innovative engineering solutions, including a lightweight frame, flexible mesh wheels, and a collapsible design for efficient transport. These innovations had applications in other fields, such as lightweight vehicle design and foldable structures. Telecommunications: The Moon Buggy was equipped with advanced telecommunications systems that allowed for data transmission back to Earth. These systems contributed to the development of space communication technology, which has since been used in various space missions and satellite communications.
