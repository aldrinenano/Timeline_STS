Yo-yo

Pedro Flores, 1920. Although its origins are debated, the yo-yo has a long history in the Philippines and is known as a traditional Filipino toy. Materials Science: The design and materials used in yo-yos have evolved over time to enhance performance. This evolution has contributed to advancements in materials science, including the development of high-performance plastics and precision machining. Engineering: The design and engineering of yo-yos have become more sophisticated with innovations like ball bearings and adjustable gap widths. These innovations have led to advancements in precision engineering, particularly in the manufacturing of small, precision parts.
