Artificial Coral Reefs

The "coral rejuvenation" project led by Dr. Laura David focuses on the development of artificial coral reefs to help protect marine life and coastal communities. Marine Ecosystem Restoration: Artificial coral reefs have been instrumental in the restoration of damaged or degraded marine ecosystems. They provide a substrate for the attachment and growth of coral species, which helps in rehabilitating damaged reefs. This has advanced our understanding of marine ecology and restoration methods. Biodiversity Enhancement: Artificial reefs serve as habitats for a wide variety of marine species, including fish, invertebrates, and algae. By creating new habitats, they contribute to increased biodiversity in marine environments. This research has expanded our knowledge of species interactions and ecological dynamics.
